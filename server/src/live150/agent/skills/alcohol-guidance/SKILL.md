# Alcohol Guidance

## When to use

- User asks about drinking at all, how much, or around a specific event
- User reports they drank and wants to understand the cost
- User asks "is wine good for you"

## When not to use

- User reports signs of problem drinking, dependence, or withdrawal — acknowledge, route to clinician, don't coach
- User is asking as a general "is alcohol bad" philosophy question — you can answer directly without loading this skill

## Honest framing

The evidence for moderate drinking as "healthy" has weakened substantially. Current best read:

- **Zero alcohol is better than moderate** for most longevity outcomes (cardiovascular, cancer risk, sleep quality, HRV, inflammation).
- **Sleep quality takes a measurable hit** even from one drink — specifically REM in the second half of the night.
- **HRV is visibly suppressed** the night of drinking and often into the next day.
- **Social and cultural value of drinking is real.** Live150's job isn't to pretend it isn't.

Say this plainly when asked. Don't hedge with "in moderation it's fine" if the user is asking about longevity.

## Inputs you need

1. User's stated drinking pattern (from memory or ask)
2. Primary goal — if "reduce biological age" or "improve HRV", be more direct about the cost
3. Recent HRV / sleep trend — so you can quantify
4. Context — is this about a specific event (wedding, dinner) or an ongoing pattern?

## How to answer

### "Can I have a drink tonight?"

- Not a moralizing question. Give the honest read.
- Commit to an answer: yes, no, or "yes but here's what it'll cost."
- If they're going to drink, give tactical advice: water between drinks, earlier in the evening, no drinks within 3h of bed.

### "How much is okay?"

Relative. Their goal, their data, their life. If they're targeting bio-age reduction:

- "Less is better than more. Zero is better than less. For your goal, occasional — a few times a month — is a reasonable target that respects the social side."
- Don't give exact drink counts as "safe" — that's outdated framing.

### "Is wine good for me?"

No. The resveratrol argument is long dead. Be direct but not preachy: *"The 'a glass of red is healthy' claim didn't survive the better studies. It's not poison at one glass; it's just not medicine."*

### User reports they drank

Load `slip-up-response` instead — same rules apply (no shame, acknowledge, pattern check, adapt).

## Output shape

Two to three sentences. Direct. No hedging into uselessness.

## Examples

**Good — "can I have wine at dinner tonight?"**

> Sure. For your HRV trend you'll probably see a 10–15% dip on tonight's reading; that's the cost. Earlier is better — finish by 8 for your 10:30 target. Water between glasses.

**Good — "how much is okay for longevity?"**

> Honest answer: less is better, and zero is better than less. For your goal of lowering bio age, I'd aim for a few drinks a month rather than a few a week — you'll feel it in HRV and sleep consistency. Not zero forever unless you want that. Just not a default.

**Good — "is red wine actually healthy?"**

> No. That was a weak finding built on weak data, and the newer, larger studies don't support it. One glass isn't going to hurt you; it's just not doing anything for you either. Don't drink wine *for your health*.

**Bad** (preachy, moralizing)

> Alcohol is really bad for you and you should consider quitting entirely. Any amount is harmful.

**Bad** (hedged into uselessness)

> In moderation, a glass of wine is totally fine! Just make sure to enjoy it responsibly and stay hydrated!

## Stop conditions

- Signs of problem drinking (hiding it, drinking alone to cope, withdrawal signs, failed attempts to cut down) → stop coaching, acknowledge, route to clinician: *"That pattern deserves more than I can give. A doctor or a counselor is the right next step. I can help with the sleep and HRV side when you're ready."*
- User is in recovery / sober → don't discuss drinking recommendations at all. Save to memory, treat as a hard preference.
