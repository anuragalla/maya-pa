# Fasting Window

## When to use

- User asks about intermittent fasting, time-restricted eating, 16:8, 18:6, OMAD, fasting
- User asks when to eat their first or last meal
- User wants to try fasting for the first time
- User is already fasting and asks if they should adjust

## When not to use

- Suspected disordered eating (compulsive restriction, binge patterns, body-image distress) — do not give fasting guidance; acknowledge with care and defer to clinician
- Diabetes with insulin — requires medical coordination, don't freelance
- Pregnancy or nursing — default position is no fasting beyond overnight
- Active eating disorder history — even past — be extremely careful

## Honest framing upfront

The evidence is more mixed than the hype suggests. Be honest:

- **Time-restricted eating (12–14h overnight fast) is low-risk and has reasonable support** for metabolic and sleep benefits.
- **Longer windows (16h+) have mixed evidence**, especially for women, people under high chronic stress, and people with low body fat.
- **OMAD and multi-day fasts have real risks** and are not a default recommendation from Live150.
- The goal for most users is consistent eating windows that support sleep and energy — not ever-longer fasts.

## Inputs you need

1. User's biological sex and menstrual status if applicable (fasting has sex-specific considerations)
2. Conditions — diabetes, thyroid, eating disorder history, pregnancy
3. Current eating window if known (first meal → last meal)
4. Primary goal — metabolic health, fat loss, sleep, longevity — the answer differs
5. Stress level / training volume — heavier training and chronic stress push against longer fasts

## How to answer

### "Should I try 16:8?"

Defaults:

- **Healthy adult male, no conditions, moderate training** → start with 14:10, see how a month goes, maybe drift to 16:8 if it feels right.
- **Healthy adult female, menstruating** → start with 12:12 or 13:11. Longer windows can disrupt cycle. 16:8 is fine for many but not a default.
- **Post-menopausal** → 14:10 reasonable starting point.
- **Diabetes / prediabetes** → coordinate with clinician; small eating-window contractions can help with metabolic control but the specifics matter.
- **High stress / poor sleep / burnout** → not the right tool right now. Fasting is a mild stressor; don't add it to an already-taxed system.

### "When should I eat my first and last meal?"

- **Last meal should end ≥2 hours before bedtime** for HRV and sleep quality. This matters more than the total window length.
- **First meal after morning light and some movement** is a reasonable heuristic, not a hard rule.
- Anchor the *late boundary* first (bedtime minus 2–3h). Work back from there. Eating earlier in the day and fasting overnight is the shape that has the best evidence.

### "I'm already fasting — should I extend?"

Don't default to longer. Ask what they're solving for. If their sleep, HRV, and energy are good, hold. If they're chasing fat loss via longer fasts, check calories first; extended fasts often just shift timing without changing total intake.

## Output shape

Two to three short paragraphs. Commit to a recommendation. Don't give a buffet of options.

## Examples

**Good — "should I try 16:8?" for a stressed male user**

> Your sleep debt this week and the uptick in training volume make now a bad time to add a fasting window. Fasting is a mild stressor — your body's already handling plenty.
>
> If you want to move the timing of your eating, aim for a 7pm cutoff on dinner instead. That gets you most of the metabolic benefit without adding load. We can revisit 16:8 once the week-to-week HRV stabilizes.

**Good — "when should I eat?" for a female user, no conditions**

> Aim for your last meal to finish by 7pm for your 10pm bedtime — that two-hour gap matters more for sleep quality than any specific fasting window. First meal whenever hunger actually shows up, usually within an hour or two of waking. That'll naturally land you at a 12–13 hour overnight fast, which is the range where the evidence is cleanest for women.

**Bad** (hype, no personalization)

> Yes, 16:8 is amazing! Try to extend to 18:6 once you're comfortable. Fasting activates autophagy and reverses aging!

## Stop conditions

- **Any sign of disordered eating** → do not give fasting guidance, regardless of how the question is framed. Redirect.
- **User reports low energy, hair loss, lost cycle, cold intolerance** while fasting → flag it: *"These are signs your body isn't tolerating the fast. Back off to 12 hours and let's watch for two weeks."*
- **User is on insulin or sulfonylureas** → refuse to freelance on timing: *"Your endocrinologist has to weigh in on this. The timing question interacts with your medication in ways I shouldn't guess at."*
